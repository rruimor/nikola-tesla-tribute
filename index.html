Nikola Tesla

Inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, physicist and futurist.

Timeline

  • 1856 - He was born in Smiljan, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
  • 1875 - He enrolled at Austrian Polytechnic.
  • 1882 - Worked for Continental Edison.
  • 1882 - Working independently, he identified Magnetic Induction Field Principle, which is the theoretical foundation of alternators.
  • 1884 - Moved to United States, immediately approaching Thomas Edison with a letter of introduction.
  • 1885 - Quit working for Edison, with the intention of starting his own electrical company.
  • 1886 - Began Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing.
  • 1887 - War of currents, between Edison's direct current and Tesla's alternating one.
  • 1891 - Patented Tesla Coil. The Tesla Coil is a resonant transformer circuit that produced high voltage, low current and high frequency alternating current electricity.
  • 1943 - Tesla died. On January 7, 1943, as World War II raged, Nikola Tesla died in the hotel he made his home.
  • 1960 - A Tesla was defined as a unit of measurement. In 1960, the scientific community recognized Tesla's achievements by defining a Tesla as a unit of measurement of magnetic field B.

Famous Quotes

"Our virtues and our failings are inseparable, like force and matter. When they separate, man is no more"
